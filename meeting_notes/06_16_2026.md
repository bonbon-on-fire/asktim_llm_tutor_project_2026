### Research design for the paper

- The core of the research design is playing with the amount of context given to the tutor.
- The goal is to see whether giving more context — and which types of context — makes the tutor behave like a better tutor.
- Evaluation will mostly happen through simulated conversations and grading.
- As part of expanding the context, the team will add the 11.270x lecture transcript into the tutor context.

### Context expansion

- Discuss rolling image input out to the deployed app.
- All 11.270x lecture transcripts have been added into the tutor context, but this has not been run/tested yet.
- The full lecture set is ~99,950 words (~612,340 characters), which adds ~125,600 tokens of context to every tutor call.
- This raises the cost per message by roughly 17× — from about 1¢ to about 17¢ — which can add up quickly in longer conversations, so it was flagged to Romain and Faizan.
- Romain's take: this is fine for testing purposes for now; if the lecture context proves genuinely useful, the team will look at bringing the cost down — in practice by using RAG to pull only the relevant chunks of the lectures rather than the full transcripts.

### Next focus areas

- It is now time to put a lot more focus on literature review for LLM tutors in general.
- The team should also focus on the literature and methods for simulating students using LLMs, since simulated conversations are central to the evaluation plan.
