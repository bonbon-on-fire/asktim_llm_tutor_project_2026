City Case Study Investigations — Case Study Stressors

Recommended due date: June 20 by 11:59 pm EST. Final due date: August 29 by 11:59 PM EST.

This assignment is required for Certificate learners, but all learners are encouraged to complete the City Case Study Investigations.

This assignment will be reviewed by the course instructors for content, and will not include direct instructor feedback. A discussion forum will be offered to allow you to exchange and discuss findings with your fellow learners, for peer learning.

Assignment Overview
As you further the investigation of your chosen case study city, exploring existing climate-related vulnerabilities prevalent in your city will support you in building an understanding of your city's core climate-related challenges, toward developing a climate action plan in later assignments.

Assignment Tasks
You will complete a City Case Study Stressors Table to record your city's key climate-related vulnerabilities.

Step 1: Identify Vulnerabilities Across Multiple Sectors
Consider vulnerabilities across the following sectors:
• Environmental
• Social
• Health
• Infrastructure
• Culture

Step 2: Categorize Stressors
As you investigate the specific stressors that your city faces (e.g. coastal flooding, air quality, extreme heat), list them in your table based on whether the stressor is:
• Environmental
• Social
• Health
• Infrastructure
• Culture-related
• Or a combination of multiple sectors

Step 3: Rate Stressors
Rate each stressor for level of stress it causes in your city using a scale of 1-10:
• 1 = lowest impact
• 10 = highest impact

Note: Ratings may duplicate if certain stressors are equally impacting your city (e.g. coastal flooding and sea level rise might both be rated as 10).

Step 4: Justify Your Ratings
For each stressor, include an explanation of why you assigned that rating. What evidence or conditions support this severity level?

Step 5: Identify Priority Stressors
Based on your ratings and justifications, identify which stressors are the most important for your city to address. Consider both severity and urgency.

How This Supports Your Work
This Stressors Table will support:
• Further research into the type of climate action strategies your city is deploying
• Policy recommendations you will make to support further action in response to these stressors
