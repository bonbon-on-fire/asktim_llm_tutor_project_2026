PROFESSOR: The years 2020, 2021, and the beginning of 2022
will be associated as COVID-19 pandemic years for years
to come and will likely appear in a variety of history books
yet to be printed.
2020, 2021, and early '22 are especially
relevant for New York City, which was amongst the earliest
impacted cities.
In the research paper, "The Compound Risk
of Heat and COVID-19 in New York City, Riskscapes, Physical
and Social Factors and Interventions,"
we take a look at the timing of each of the three COVID-19
waves in relation to public health and city interventions.
The first COVID-19 was a summer wave
making the correspondence of heat and COVID-19 risk
most prominent.
During the first wave, New York officials issued a mandatory
two-and-a-half-month stay-at-home order
and face-mask mandates.
The second COVID-19 wave was characterized
by standardized masking, social-distancing mandates,
increased testing, and vaccination rollout.
Moving into the third wave, widespread testing
and vaccinations continued.
In fact, many New Yorkers, especially prioritized groups
such as essential workers and individuals over the age of 65,
had received their first vaccination
by the start of the third wave.
