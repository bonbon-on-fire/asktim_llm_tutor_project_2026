PROFESSOR 1: In order to create stakeholder-informed research
on the compound risk of heat and COVID-19
in New York City, riskscapes, physical and social factors,
and interventions, we hosted multiple virtual sessions
with New York City stakeholders during the COVID-19 pandemic.
We consulted with city stakeholders
from the Mayor's Office of Resiliency,
the Department of Health and Mental Hygiene,
the Department of City Planning, the Department
of Transportation, and the Department
of Parks and Recreation.
Based on these discussions, we explored how the interventions
conducted in the summer of 2020, such as cool streets
and open streets, related to the compound riskscapes on heat
exposure and COVID-19.
PROFESSOR 2: In future work related to compound risk
indices and understanding compound risk,
we would like to extend the scope and scale of our analysis
to include predominant climate change-related environmental
effects in New York City, including sea level
rise, coastal flooding, and storm surge risks,
as well as ambient air quality risks.
We can then integrate these factors
into the compound risk scale.
We would like to use this methodology
to examine compound risks in other cities
beyond New York City.
Multi-city comparison would give us
a holistic analysis of the trends and impacts
of the COVID-19 pandemic across multiple waves
and allow us to catalog a variety of city interventions
and inactions.
This analysis can likewise be replicated
at larger scales across the state of New York,
for example, to understand more complex urban-rural dynamics.
Taken together, these techniques, findings,
and avenues of future research offer new ways
to understand compound climate and pandemic risks,
and productive new strategies to develop related
to disaster risk reduction.
