MARIA DOMBROV: Hello, my name is Maria Dombrov,

I oversee UCCRN's 15 regional hubs, ongoing projects
and publications, and research tools such as the case study
docking station.
UCCRN has partnered with MIT'S Environmental Solutions
Initiative to create this rich resource in 2018 that
is available for students, researchers, and decision
makers.
But what is the case study docking station?
It's an online, searchable, open source database
with over 100 peer reviewed case studies from cities
all around the world.
These city case studies cover a range of topics,
such as climate change vulnerability, hazards,
impacts, mitigation and adaptation actions,
and sector specific themes such as wastewater and flood
management.
In these case studies, authors identify localized climate
risks, or sources of greenhouse gas emissions,
and offer potential strategies that are often transferable
to other cities.
Case study cities include Ahmedabad, Cairo, Melbourne,
Paris, New York City, and Rio de Janeiro,
just to name a handful.

docking station by simply googling
the words "case study docking station", or clicking
