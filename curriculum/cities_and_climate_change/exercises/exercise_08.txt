Assignment Overview
This week you will complete a Spider Diagram to map out climate action in your case study city as it relates to the 6 Pathways for Urban Transformation.

Assignment Options
The Spider Diagram activity can be completed in two different ways, which will be described in detail on the following pages:
- Option 1: Use the Spider Diagram table (see example on next pages) to write out the information you find about your city (preferred).
  - To complete this option, you will copy and paste a table template into your submission
- Option 2: Use the Spider Diagram diagram (see example on next pages) to draw out the information you find about your city.
  - To complete this option, you will download, print and complete the map by hand, then scan it in to add it to your submission. Alternatively, you may electronically draw directly on the digital file (for example with a tablet and stylus) and upload it.

After you submit EITHER your Spider Diagram table OR your Spider Diagram diagram to the Graded Response, you will also be required to participate in the Discussion Panel with your fellow learners. Finally, you will give feedback to fellow learners (next week) via peer review.

Spider Diagram Table instructions

SPIDER DIAGRAM TABLE instructions

Before completing your table:

REFER TO YOUR PRIOR RESEARCH AND SPECIFICALLY YOUR FINDINGS FROM WEEK 7.

As you gather information to complete your Spider Diagram Table or Visual Diagram, ask yourself the following questions - (1) Is your city initiating action for all the 6 Pathways for Urban Transformation, especially addressing both mitigation and adaptation with active programs? And (2) is there a strong commitment within your city's leadership that is engendering a cultural shift towards responding to climate change (and its larger umbrella, sustainability) with concerted efforts to engage on these issues with the entire city?

To help you determine if your city is initiating actions regarding the 6 Pathways for Urban Transformation, consider the following:

Pathway 1: Integrate mitigation and adaptation:
- Have you found plans, projects, portfolios or investment commitments, integrating mitigation and adaptation?

Pathway 2: Coordinate disaster risk reduction and climate change adaptation:
- Have you found studies, plans or protocols, to prepare or recover from disasters that consider the direct implications of climate change?

Pathway 3: Co-generate risk information:
- Who have you found has been engaged in planning/design, decision-making, implementation and/or oversight of climate action?

Pathway 4: Focus on disadvantage populations:
- Is there an emphasis on the needs/priorities of population living in vulnerable locations or disproportionately burdened by historic/future environmental impacts?

Pathway 5: Advance governance, finance and knowledge networks:
- Have you found internal/external actors/institutions leveraging technical/financial resources?

Pathway 6: Equity and Environmental Justice:
- Have you found strategies or initiatives, projects or campaigns in your city's planning that address environmental and climate justice and equity?

To complete your table:

When filling out the Spider Diagram Table, please use the following as a guide:
- Stages of Progress (from Assessment to Full Transformation) refer to the status of climate action initiatives and strategies in your city. You are investigating whether climate action for each Pathway is in the Assessmnet phase, has begun Initial Implementation, etc.
- Pathways refer to the 6 Pathways for Urban Transformation that you learned about in Week 5.
- For each Pathway you will score climate action progress for your city by placing an 'X' in the cell corresponding to Assessment, Planning, Initial Implementation, etc).

Instructions on Completing a Spider Diagram
To complete a Spider Diagram diagram, follow the steps below:
- Download and print the SPIDER DIAGRAM TEMPLATE
- Each rung of the circle (labeled 1-5) corresponds to the 5 stages of progress from 1. Assessment, 2. Planning, 3. Initial Implementation, 4. Implementation and Evaluation, 5. Full Transformation.
- The colors filled in each refer to the 6 Pathways for Urban Transformation, as seen in the Diagram legend.
- For each Pathway (occupying 1 segment of the circle per pathway), color in your Spider Diagram to fill the number of rungs (1-5) that your city has progressed for each Pathway.
  - For example, the Diagram above shows that this example city has progressed to Stage 4 (Implementation and Evaluation) for Pathway 6 (Equity and Environmental Justice), shown by the 4 Orange segments that have been filled in.
- Once you have completed your Spider Diagram diagram, you will upload it to your Graded Response (following pages).

Use the Discussion Forum (following pages) to share your findings with fellow learners. Which Pathway and its related climate action initiatives show the most progress for your city? Why do you believe this is the case? How has this visual diagram helped you to picture where climate action is most effective?
