Instructions for Filling Out the Table

When filling out the table, please use the following as a guide:

- Actor: The roles you've identified as key players in your city.
  - In the example city above, we see the actors of City Mayor, City Planners, Environmental NGO, and Transportation Advocate.
- Actor Role: Can be one of three options:
  - decision-maker: a decision-maker is seen as a key figure in climate action in your city, and someone who has the power to make critical policy-based decisions (i.e. a Mayor)
  - other involved actor: other involved actors are seen as important figures in climate action who are involved in influencing decision-making but who don't have the power to make final decisions (i.e. urban planner)
  - relevant but not involved: relevant but uninvolved actors are seen as important figures in climate action who are not being included in the decision-making process but who are relevant to its progress (i.e. transporation advocates)
  - In the example city above, the City Mayor is considered a decision-maker, as they have significant power in what laws are passed which may protect or harm the environment. The Transportation Advocate, however, is relevant to the discussions around climate action, but has not yet been made more fully involved in climate action-related decisions.
- Opposing / Supporting Climate Action: rank the actor's opposition or support of climate action from 0-10, with 0 being "Opposed to" and 10 being "Strongly in support of" climate action
- Engagement in Climate Action: rank the actor's enagement in climate action from 0-10, with 0 being "Not involved at all" and 10 being "Very involved in" climate action
  - In the example city above, the City Planners are both very supportive of and very involved in climate engagement. However, the Mayor, who is still fairly supportive of climate action, is only invovled in conversations around climate action about half of the time.
- Actor connected to...: What other actors that you listed is this actor already connected to in terms of climate action?
- Actor not yet connected to...: What other actors that you listed is this actor NOT yet connected to in terms of climate action?
  - In the example city above, we see that the Mayor is connected to the City Planners, but has not yet been connected to the environmental NGO, which may have important resources for the mayor to consider when making decisions.

Instructions for filling out the Diagram

POWER/ACTORS Diagram Instructions

Follow the steps below to complete your Power/Actors Map Diagram, using the included downloadable template.

- Review your decision-making actors research and use this to plot these actors on the Power/Actors Map.
  - Plot in blue the actors that are currently involved in climate action in your city. Place them in the map's circles (light to dark grey) according to whether they are decision-makers (light grey), other involved actors (medium grey) or actors not currently involved (dark grey).
    - Label these actors in the diagram
  - Plot in red actors that are missing from the diagram but should be there
    - Label these actors in the diagram
  - Plot actors based on their level of engagement (North/South part of the diagram) as well as whether they are in favor of or oppose climate action (East/West part of the diagram)
    - Label these actors in the diagram
- Next, assess the connections and power dynamics between decision making actors
  - Use SOLID lines to connect actors that are already working together, and DASHED lines to highlight new connections that should be made
  - Assess the match of organizations and actors that have influence over climate action.
