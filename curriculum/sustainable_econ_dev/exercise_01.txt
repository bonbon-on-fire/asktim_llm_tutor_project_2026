Research Plan — Viet-AID / Fields Corner

Your team is working with Viet-AID, a grassroots CDC serving Vietnamese refugees and immigrants in the Fields Corner neighborhood of Dorchester, Boston. Viet-AID's goals for this project are to:
- identify strategies for new business start-up and expansion to increase employment opportunities and create assets and wealth;
- identify strategies to work with auto-related businesses in the area that result in positive environmental and economic impacts; and
- build capacity of Vietnamese entrepreneurs to incorporate environmentally and economically sound business practices.

Fields Corner is one of the most diverse and lowest-income neighborhoods in Boston — 1.2 square miles, home to 20,000 residents and 60+ Vietnamese businesses. Serious environmental pollution results from the many auto-related uses in the area. Disinvestment and lack of sound economic infrastructure have contributed to the economic blight of the neighborhood.

Assignment: Each team (or a designated sub-group) should discuss the following questions, prepare a brief (2-page) synopsis of your research plan, and be prepared to discuss your research approach in class.

1. Which environmental and economic conditions are most important to evaluate for the neighborhood, project, and related sustainability goals? What are appropriate indicators or measures to use to report and track these conditions?

2. What information is already available in the project background materials?

3. What additional information is needed?

4. What data sources exist for this information? Which are most relevant and feasible given the project geography and time frame?

5. What is the research plan to compile and analyze this data in the next 2 to 3 weeks?
