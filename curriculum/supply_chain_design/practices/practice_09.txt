Practice Problem 1: MacKenzie Markets
Mackenzie Markets operates a vast chain of convenience stores. It recently leased a medium-sized warehouse for 900,000 dollars per year in an urban location to support small, frequent deliveries. The appointed warehouse manager, with a salary of 180,000, is considering acquisition of automation equipment with small robots that move merchandise around the warehouse. The equipment costs 2,000,000 and the accountants will use straight-line depreciation over 10 years. Given the short-term lease, Mackenzie is evaluating this initiative under the assumption that they sell the equipment at book value after three years.
The equipment would enable Mackenzie to reduce warehouse labor cost by 450,000. With this equipment, Mackenzie could cancel the lease of forklifts and some other material handling equipment having an annual cost of 350,000. Finally, given the location of the warehouse, transportation to the urban stores would be more efficient, reducing costs by 300,000 annually.
Some Mackenzie Markets technical staff, with annual salaries totaling 250,000, will be moved from a warehouse location where the robotic implementation went so well that they were no longer needed. They recently completed training on the robotic equipment controls systems at a cost of 40,000. Finally, the implementation would require Mackenzie to hire a floor manager and some maintenance staff with robotic experience at a combined annual salary of 300,000.
Assume the tax rate is 30% and the discount rate is 10%. Ignore inflation.
