Practice Problem 1: Procurement at SmallCo
A small company, SmallCo, has just hired you for your expertise in purchasing. The idea is to have you go over and renegotiate all supplier contracts. Last year, SmallCo had revenues of $10M, COGS of $6M, sales and general costs of $2M, and other operating expenses of $1M.
The procurement manager has looked through all contracts and estimates that you should be able to reduce this year's COGS by as much as 10% compared to last year.
Part 1
0 points possible (ungraded)
How much would the percent increase in net income (before interest and taxes) be if you reduce COGS by 10% next year, assuming all other costs/revenues remain the same? Answer in full percent without percentage symbol, e.g. if your answer is 30%, write 30.
Part 2
0 points possible (ungraded)
Uplifted by the potential impact of your actions, you start preparing to renegotiate the contracts for a key component, responsible for 17% of COGS. After sending out requests for proposals to a number of suppliers, two suppliers return with bids and the information you required. Neither has previously been hired by SmallCo.
As you look through the two bids you see that they differ, in particular, in terms of handling requirements and delivery reliability.
Supplier 1 is 10% cheaper than the current supplier, but uses a different type of transport package than what your warehouse is designed for. You know that this potential problem can be handled through a small investment in equipment that transforms the package when it arrives at the warehouse. Going through this step at each arrival, however, leads to additional handling costs of $100,000 per year. The investment, if made, will be paid for by loans with total interest payments of $60,000 per year.
Supplier 2 is 10% cheaper than Supplier 1, but less reliable than Supplier 1 and the current supplier, leading to increased safety stocks costing $175,000 per year.
Which supplier should you choose to maximize net income?
Select the best answer
Supplier 1
Supplier 2
Neither -- try to stick with current supplier
Part 3
0 points possible (ungraded)
You decide to go with the supplier that leads to the highest net income. What is the net income (before taxes) with this supplier, ceteris paribus? Answer in dollars without commas or currency symbols, e.g. if your answer is $4M, write 4000000.
Part 4
0 points possible (ungraded)
What is the percent change in net income from renegotiating this single contract? Answer in percent with one decimal without the percentage symbol, e.g. if your answer is 15.55% then write 15.6
Part 5
0 points possible (ungraded)
Three carriers submit bids. Carrier I (your current carrier) offers a flat rate of $2 per mile, Carrier II offers a flat rate of $1 per mile and an additional index-based surcharge of $0.02 per index point and mile, and Carrier III offers a flat rate of $1.4 per mile and an index-based surcharge of $0.01 per index point and mile.
The transports for the next year will total 500,000 miles. Today the index is 47. The contract will be a one year contract.
Which carrier should you choose to maximize net income?
Select the best answer
Carrier I
Carrier II
Carrier III
Part 6
0 points possible (ungraded)
The index has been stable lately but is expected to rise.
At what index level above 47 would the optimal carrier choice change?
Part 7
0 points possible (ungraded)
Assuming the index stays at 47, what is the percent change in net income compared to the original net income (1 million)? Assume the key component contract selected in Part 2 and the transportation contract selected in Part 5 are in place. Answer in percent with one decimal, without the percentage symbol, e.g. if your answer is 15.55%, write 15.6.

Bids (Capacity)

S-N	100 (8)	120 (10)	40 (1)

Practice Problem 4: YouKea
Remember Youkea, the upcoming Scandinavian furniture retailer?
As you may remember, Youkea has three stores located in Boston, Providence and New Haven, and has, in anticipation of future growth, just opened a small distribution center in Springfield, MA. Youkea plans to ship 10 truckloads per week to each store from the distribution center and now needs help in the procurement of these transports.
The regional supply chain director is currently deciding on which carriers to contract for the shipments from the distribution center to the stores. A few months ago she sent out a request for proposals (RFPs) and now three carriers have submitted proposals: BigLoad, Shipfast, and TruckerCo. BigLoad and Shipfast are large nationwide carriers, whereas TruckerCo is a small family-run company that mainly focuses on transports from the Boston area to Springfield and now sees a good opportunity to balance its loads and grow its business.
The bids (price per truckload) and carrier capacity (truckloads per week) are given in the table below.
Bids (Capacity)
Lane	BigLoad	ShipFast	TruckerCo
S-B	110 (10)	100 (12)	90 (8)
S-P	70 (10)	80 (12)	80 (1)
S-N	100 (8)	120 (10)	120 (1)
Part 1
0 points possible (ungraded)
Build an optimization model in your spreadsheet software to help Youkea.
Based on the above information, what is the minimum cost for the network (subject to the capacity constraints)? Answer in integer currency units without decimals or commas.
Part 2
0 points possible (ungraded)
After some research, the director finds out that the on-time performance of the carriers differ. The results of her investigations are seen in the table below.
Service Level
Lane	BigLoad	ShipFast	TruckerCo
S-B	95%	97%	98%
S-P	95%	96%	97%
S-N	95%	95%	90%
The director thinks that %1 service is roughly equal to $10 per truckload.
What is the minimum LOS-adjusted cost, if we normalize LOS at 95%? Answer in full currency units without decimals or commas.
Part 3
0 points possible (ungraded)
What is the actual cost with the minimum LOS-adjusted solution? Answer in full currency units without decimals or commas.
Part 4
0 points possible (ungraded)
After thinking about the solution and talking to the carriers, the director believes there are strategic reasons to allocate a larger portion of the business with the smaller carrier (TruckerCo). She believes that TruckerCo should be providing transports on each lane.
What is the minimum actual cost for the LOS-adjusted solution after this change? Answer in full currency units without decimals or commas.

After preliminary talks with the third supplier, that supplier too has accepted the format and submitted bids.
All bids (expressed as price per "package"), and demand volumes, are found in this Excel-file or this LibreOffice-file. A package can be seen as a product or a combination of products.
ProducerGambler wants to optimize the procurement so that products are purchased at minimum cost.
Part 1
0 points possible (ungraded)

What is the cost of the optimal solution?
Round your answer to the nearest integer without currency symbols.
Part 2
0 points possible (ungraded)
