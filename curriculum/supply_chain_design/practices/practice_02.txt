Practice Problem 6: TIme-Expanded Network
In this problem, we will consider the following formulation of a time-expanded network problem.
Min \( z = \sum_{i} \sum_{j} \sum_{t} c_{ijt} x_{ijt} + \sum_{j} \sum_{t} h I_{jt} \)
s.t.
\( \sum_{j} x_{ijt} \leq S_{it} \quad \forall i \in S, t \in T \)
\( \sum_{i} x_{ijt} \geq D_{jt} \quad \forall j \in D, t \in T \)
\( \sum_{i} x_{ijt} - \sum_{i} x_{jit} + I_{j,t-1} - I_{jt} = 0 \quad \forall j \notin S, t \in T \)
\( I_{jt} = I_{jt}^{initial} \quad \forall j, t = 0 \)
\( x_{ijt} \geq 0 \quad \forall i,j,t \)

In time-expanded models, we investigate the system across multiple time periods. This approach allows products within the model to move not just spatially but also temporally. Contrary to the standard transport or transshipment models, time-expanded models allow for explicit incorporation of inventory holding. In the formulation mentioned, \(h\) represents the inventory holding cost.
