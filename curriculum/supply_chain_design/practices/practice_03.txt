Practice Problem 1: MPS for Electronic Widgets
An electronic widget manufacturer is about to plan its production of a high selling widget, NOX12K678P. The demand for the widget for the next 10 weeks has been estimated as follows:
Week	Demand
1	200
2	180
3	220
4	170
5	160
6	170
7	180
8	190
9	220
10	210
TOTAL	1900
As soon as a widget is produced it is sent to the finished goods (FG) inventory. If it is shipped from the FG inventory the same week it is produced, the manufacturer assumes no inventory holding cost for the widget. However, if a widget is not shipped from the inventory the same week it is produced, a holding cost of $1 per widget is incurred for every week in inventory. As of now (end of week 0), there are 300 widgets in the FG inventory.
The setup cost to run a batch is assumed to be $1,200. The manufacturer could make all widgets in one week, so capacity is not an issue. Note that setup costs are incurred for the first batch every week independently of whether the same unit was produced in the last run of the previous week.
Part 1
0 points possible (ungraded)
What is the total cost to produce the 10 week demand for NOX12K678P if the manufacturer chooses a fixed level manufacturing strategy for the MPS? Assume that each week has one production run. Please give your answer in dollars without commas or currency symbols.
Part 2
0 points possible (ungraded)
What is the total cost to produce the 10 week demand for NOX12K678P if the manufacturer chooses a Chase Demand manufacturing strategy for the MPS? The demand is considered deterministic so there is no need for a safety stock. Please give your answer in dollars without commas or currency symbols.
Part 3
0 points possible (ungraded)
The manufacturer realizes it may be a better idea to use some kind of hybrid strategy taking into account both holding cost and setup costs.
What is the total cost to produce the 10 week demand for NOX12K678P if the manufacturer uses the Silver-Meal heuristic? Please give your answer in dollars without commas or currency symbols.
Part 4
0 points possible (ungraded)
Formulate a MILP-spreadsheet to find the optimal production plan.
In the optimal solution, what is the total cost to produce the 10 week demand for NOX12K678P? Please give your answer in dollars without commas or currency symbols.

Practice Problem 2: Sodaco
Sodaco runs a plant that makes and bottles private label soft drinks, sold directly to retailers. One client has recently placed an order for one of the SKUs with the following delivery schedule over the next ten weeks (one period=one week):
Period	Demand
1	3000
2	3100
3	3200
4	3100
5	2900
6	2800
7	2800
8	2900
9	2800
10	3000
TOTAL	29600
The order is of one-time character - there are no promises for future orders from the same client.
Sodaco needs your help to create a production plan that minimizes the total costs. Costs are incurred both from holding inventory of finished goods and from changeovers in the production line. Sodaco estimates these costs according to the following:
Inventory holding costs: $0.1 per item and week
Production setup costs: $800 per production run
Any products delivered in the same week as manufactured incur no holding costs. Holding costs are then incurred for every week after the manufacturing week. Capacity is not an issue at this point.
Part 1
Using the One-time-run heuristic, what is the total cost for producing the ordered units? Please give your answer in dollars without commas or currency symbols.
Part 2
Using the Lot-for-lot heuristic, what is the total cost for producing the ordered units? Please give your answer in dollars without commas or currency symbols.
Part 3
One way to improve the solutions from Part 1 and Part 2 would be by using the FOQ heuristic. Use the EOQ-formula to find the optimal production quantity. Use average demand (2,960). Round your answer to nearest integer.
Using the FOQ heuristic, what is the total cost for producing the ordered units? Please give your answer in dollars without commas or currency symbols.
Part 4
You also want to try the POQ heuristic. Assume the optimal time between runs, \(t^*\), is 4.
Using the POQ heuristic, what is the total cost for producing the ordered units? Please give your answer in dollars without commas or currency symbols.
Part 5
The Silver-Meal heuristic provides a slightly more advanced way to create a production schedule.
Using the Silver-Meal heuristic, what is the total cost for producing the ordered units? Please give your answer in dollars without commas or currency symbols.
Part 6
While the previous algorithms provide only heuristic solutions, there are several ways to find the optimal solution. For a small problem like this, the fastest way is arguably to use a spreadsheet model. Please make such a model.
According to your model, what is the minimum total cost for producing the ordered units? Please give your answer in dollars without commas or currency symbols.

Practice Problem 3: Custom Glass Inc.
Custom Glass Inc. makes custom-made windows and window frames for all types of buildings. They have just received an order from a client to make and deliver 1,340 framed windows. The windows, which are identical to one another, are to be delivered over the course of six months, according to the following schedule:
January: 300 windows
February: 280 windows
March: 200 windows
April: 190 windows
May: 160 windows
June: 210 windows
To avoid tying up too much working capital, the production manager of Custom Glass, Mr. Dumpty, is hesitant to produce all 1,340 windows at once. Not only would this tie up capital, it would also lead to a high risk obsolescence due to the rough handling in the warehouse. However, splitting up the order into several batches is also costly. For every batch they make there will be an initial setup cost due to equipment changeover and scrap from the "burn-in" period when the equipment is being fine-tuned.
Mr. Dumpty estimates the unit holding cost to be $2 per month and the setup cost to be $1,000 per batch. If the windows are shipped within the same month, they do not incur any holding costs, but for future periods they do. Custom Glass can easily produce all windows in a day, so capacity is not an issue. The factory closes at night, so you can assume there is a setup cost for the first batch every day, independently of whether it was the last batch the day before or not.
Mr. Dumpty now asks for your help in making a production plan for the order.
