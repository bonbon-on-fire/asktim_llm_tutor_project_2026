Final Paper

Write a final paper not exceeding 20 double-spaced pages (excluding references). The paper must adhere to academic integrity standards and use Chicago Manual of Style citations throughout.

Your paper should engage substantively with the themes, theories, and debates covered in the course — such as state intervention, governance, participatory planning, decentralization, poverty, corruption, trade and labor standards, or post-conflict development — applied to a specific country, context, or planning problem of your choosing. The argument should be original, clearly structured, and grounded in evidence from course readings and additional research.
